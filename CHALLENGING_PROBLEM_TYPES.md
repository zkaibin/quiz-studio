## New Problem Types to Add

### 1. **Chicken and Rabbit Problems (Assumption Method)**
Classic Singapore Math problems involving two types of items with different attributes.
- **Examples:**
  - Chickens and rabbits with different number of legs
  - Bicycles and tricycles with different number of wheels
  - Adults and children with different ticket prices
- **Skills:** Algebra thinking, systematic approach, making assumptions
- **Difficulty:** Challenging to PSLE
- **Number of questions to create:** 8-10

### 2. **Excess and Shortage Problems**
Problems where different grouping methods result in excess or shortage.
- **Examples:**
  - Grouping students in groups of 4 vs groups of 5
  - Distributing items with different amounts leftover or needed
  - Packing problems with remainders
- **Skills:** Simultaneous thinking, difference analysis
- **Difficulty:** Challenging to PSLE
- **Number of questions to create:** 8-10

### 3. **Pattern Recognition & Number Sequences**
Problems requiring identification of patterns and extending sequences.
- **Examples:**
  - Arithmetic sequences
  - Geometric patterns
  - Figure patterns (increasing shapes)
  - Number patterns with multiple operations
- **Skills:** Pattern recognition, logical reasoning, prediction
- **Difficulty:** Challenging to PSLE
- **Number of questions to create:** 10-12

### 4. **Tree Planting Problems (Interval Problems)**
Problems involving spacing and counting in linear or circular arrangements.
- **Examples:**
  - Trees planted along a road with spacing
  - Poles along a path (closed vs open arrangements)
  - Flags on a track
  - Lamp posts on both sides of a street
- **Skills:** Spatial reasoning, understanding endpoints
- **Difficulty:** Challenging to PSLE
- **Number of questions to create:** 8-10

### 5. **Age Problems**
Complex age-related word problems involving multiple people across different time periods.
- **Examples:**
  - Sum and difference of ages at different times
  - Ratios of ages changing over time
  - Age relationships between 3+ people
- **Skills:** Algebraic thinking, working backwards, ratio concepts
- **Difficulty:** Challenging to PSLE
- **Number of questions to create:** 8-10

### 6. **Speed, Distance & Time (Advanced)**
Complex motion problems with multiple segments or multiple people.
- **Examples:**
  - Meeting problems (traveling towards each other)
  - Catching up problems (one faster than the other)
  - Average speed with different segments
  - Round trip problems
- **Skills:** Formula application, proportional reasoning
- **Difficulty:** PSLE
- **Number of questions to create:** 10-12

### 7. **Area and Perimeter Problems**
Complex geometry problems involving composite shapes and relationships.
- **Examples:**
  - Finding unknown dimensions from given area/perimeter
  - Overlapping shapes
  - Shaded region problems
  - Relationship between area and perimeter
- **Skills:** Geometric reasoning, algebraic manipulation
- **Difficulty:** Challenging to PSLE
- **Number of questions to create:** 8-10

### 8. **Volume and Capacity Problems**
Problems involving 3D shapes, water transfer, and conversions.
- **Examples:**
  - Water transfer between containers
  - Finding dimensions from volume
  - Melting/reforming shapes
  - Comparing volumes of different shapes
- **Skills:** 3D visualization, unit conversion, proportional reasoning
- **Difficulty:** PSLE
- **Number of questions to create:** 6-8

### 9. **Work Rate Problems**
Problems involving completion rates and combined work.
- **Examples:**
  - A can complete work in X days, B in Y days, together?
  - Filling/emptying pool problems with multiple pipes
  - Printing/production rate problems
- **Skills:** Rate understanding, fraction concepts, collaborative work
- **Difficulty:** Challenging to PSLE
- **Number of questions to create:** 6-8

### 10. **Before-After Concept (Transfer Problems)**
Problems where quantities are transferred between parties and ratios/amounts change.
- **Examples:**
  - Transfer of money/items changing ratios
  - Equal amounts after transfer
  - Multiple transfers with changing relationships
- **Skills:** Simultaneous equation thinking, ratio changes
- **Difficulty:** Challenging to PSLE
- **Number of questions to create:** 8-10

### 11. **Remainder Problems**
Problems involving division with remainders and Chinese Remainder Theorem concepts.
- **Examples:**
  - Finding numbers with specific remainder patterns
  - Sharing with remainders
  - Page numbering with remainders
- **Skills:** Division concepts, systematic trial
- **Difficulty:** Challenging
- **Number of questions to create:** 6-8

### 12. **Fraction of Remainder**
Classic Singapore Math problems involving taking fractions of what remains.
- **Examples:**
  - Spent 1/4, then 1/3 of remainder, then 1/2 of what's left
  - Multi-step fraction problems with successive operations
- **Skills:** Fraction operations, working backwards
- **Difficulty:** Challenging to PSLE
- **Number of questions to create:** 8-10

### 13. **Repeated Identity / Equal Quantities**
Problems where the same quantity is expressed in different ways.
- **Examples:**
  - A has 3 times what B has; A has 20 more than B
  - Finding overlap in different descriptions
- **Skills:** Algebraic reasoning, equation setup
- **Difficulty:** Challenging
- **Number of questions to create:** 6-8

### 14. **Purchasing Problems (Systems of Equations)**
Problems involving multiple purchases with different combinations.
- **Examples:**
  - 3 apples + 2 oranges = $X, 2 apples + 4 oranges = $Y
  - Buying combinations of items with total costs
- **Skills:** System solving, elimination method
- **Difficulty:** Challenging to PSLE
- **Number of questions to create:** 6-8

### 15. **Ratio & Proportion (Advanced)**
Complex ratio problems with multiple changes and relationships.
- **Examples:**
  - Three-way ratios with changes
  - Ratio of ratios
  - Combining different ratio information
- **Skills:** Ratio manipulation, equivalence
- **Difficulty:** Challenging to PSLE
- **Number of questions to create:** 8-10

### 16. **Percentage Change (Advanced)**
Problems involving successive percentage changes and reverse percentages.
- **Examples:**
  - Successive discounts/increases
  - Finding original value after percentage change
  - Profit/loss with percentage
- **Skills:** Percentage operations, compound changes
- **Difficulty:** PSLE
- **Number of questions to create:** 6-8

### 17. **Logic & Reasoning Problems**
Problems requiring systematic logical deduction.
- **Examples:**
  - True/false statements with constraints
  - Sudoku-style number placement
  - Who has what item (elimination)
- **Skills:** Logical deduction, systematic elimination
- **Difficulty:** Challenging
- **Number of questions to create:** 6-8

### 18. **Unchanging Total**
Problems where total remains constant while parts change.
- **Examples:**
  - Adding to one subtracts from another
  - Maintaining sum while changing ratio
- **Skills:** Conservation concepts, balance thinking
- **Difficulty:** Challenging
- **Number of questions to create:** 6-8
